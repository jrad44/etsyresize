WebP vs AVIF: Which Should You Use in 2025?

August 25, 2025 • ResizedImage Team

In 2025, two modern formats dominate image optimization: WebP and AVIF. Both are smaller and faster than legacy JPG or PNG — but which is right for your use case? Let’s compare side by side.

Quick Summary

  • WebP: Widely supported, fast to encode, balanced quality. Great default choice.
  • AVIF: Even smaller sizes and higher quality at low bitrates, but slower encoding. Best for high-res photos and media libraries.

Compression Efficiency

Tests show AVIF images are typically 20–40% smaller than WebP at equivalent visual quality. This matters for photo-heavy sites.

Browser & Platform Support (2025)

  • WebP: Supported by all major browsers, Android, iOS, and most apps.
  • AVIF: Now supported in Chrome, Safari (macOS/iOS), Firefox, and Edge. Still limited in some design tools.

Encoding & Performance

  • WebP encodes/decodes faster, good for bulk processing.
  • AVIF encoding is slower but efficient for storage or delivery at scale.

Transparency & Animation

  • Both support transparency.
  • WebP supports animations (replacement for GIFs).
  • AVIF supports animation but fewer tools implement it yet.

When to Use WebP

  • Everyday web images (blogs, e-commerce, UI graphics).
  • When compatibility and speed are priorities.

When to Use AVIF

  • High-resolution photography galleries.
  • Storage-heavy image archives (saves disk space).
  • Modern browsers only use case (progressive enhancement).

Best Practice in 2025

Use a <picture> element to serve AVIF first, WebP as fallback, and JPG/PNG as last fallback.

<picture>
  <source srcset="image.avif" type="image/avif">
  <source srcset="image.webp" type="image/webp">
  <img src="image.jpg" alt="Example" width="1200" height="800" loading="lazy">
</picture>
